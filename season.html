Seasonal Produce Guide

Spring (March to May)

Spring brings a fresh start to the growing season with tender, leafy vegetables and the first fruits of the year.

  • Asparagus: One of the first spring vegetables, asparagus is a perennial that comes back year after year.
  • Peas: Both snow peas and garden peas thrive in cool spring weather.
  • Leafy Greens: Spinach, lettuce, and arugula grow quickly in spring conditions.
  • Radishes: Fast-growing and crisp, radishes are perfect for spring gardens.
  • Strawberries: While they can produce fruit through summer, strawberries often have their first harvest in late spring.
Summer (June to August)

Summer's warmth brings a bounty of colorful fruits and vegetables, perfect for fresh eating and preserving.

  • Tomatoes: Summer's heat helps tomatoes ripen and develop their full flavor.
  • Peppers: Both sweet and hot peppers thrive in summer warmth.
  • Cucumbers: These refreshing vegetables grow quickly in summer.
  • Zucchini and Summer Squash: Known for their abundant harvests in peak summer.
  • Melons: Watermelons, cantaloupes, and honeydew melons ripen in summer heat.
  • Stone Fruits: Peaches, plums, and cherries typically ripen in summer.
Fall (September to November)

Fall brings hearty vegetables and fruits perfect for warming soups, stews, and pies.

  • Pumpkins and Winter Squash: Harvested in fall, these store well for winter use.
  • Apples: Many varieties reach peak ripeness in fall.
  • Brassicas: Broccoli, cauliflower, and Brussels sprouts prefer cooler fall temperatures.
  • Root Vegetables: Carrots, beets, and parsnips can be harvested in fall.
  • Grapes: Many grape varieties are harvested in early to mid-fall.
Winter (December to February)

Winter offers hardy greens, stored root vegetables, and bright citrus fruits to enliven the cold months.

  • Kale and Collards: These hardy greens can withstand frost and continue producing.
  • Citrus Fruits: Oranges, grapefruits, and lemons typically ripen in winter months.
  • Leeks: These mild onion relatives are often harvested throughout winter.
  • Brussels Sprouts: Can be harvested well into winter in some climates.
  • Winter Squash: While harvested in fall, many varieties store well and are consumed throughout winter.
